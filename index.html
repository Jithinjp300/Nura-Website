Frequently Asked Questions

Can I just walk in for a screening?

Yes. Walk into our centre and answer our pre-screening questionnaire to check if you are eligible for an appointment immediately. There are a few cases where certain screening tests would not be possible.

For instance, women who are pregnant cannot be screened with the CT, DEXA or a Mammography machines. Similarly, those with pacemakers or implants cannot be screened with the DEXA or Mammography machines.

What is Genetic Screening?

A Genetic Screening will give a blueprint of your body and it will let you know your Hereditary risks and Genetic Predisposition. We can let you know if you have a risk of developing a hereditary disease or cancer in future. This is once in a lifetime test as Germline Screening testing is done.

You have different package options at NURA, and each may come with a different combination of tests. Our comprehensive full-body health check-up covers tests for the following:

At NURA, our full body check-ups include cancer screening. We take special care to screen for the most common cancers in men and women in the packages we offer

What is the difference between a screening test and a diagnostic test?

A screening test examines and evaluates you for potential disease markers. That is, your screen so you can find out if there are any anomalies or abnormalities that can indicate a developing disease or a potential risk for one in the future. A diagnostic test is done when a disease is suspected and you are trying to establish its presence or absence.

  • It gives you the advantage of pre-screening support, where you can clarify all your questions.
